04.03.25 Warhammer 40K Battle Report: Indian Guard vs. Necrons – 500 Points, Kings of the Colosseum By: Vore Vore Figur Introduction In a grueling 500-point battle, the mighty Necrons led by marcellious. And the Depth Guard led by Elias, also known as the "Stink Guard," in a fight to dominate in Piriah Nexus. With both sides bringing powerful, yet contrasting forces to the table, it was a battle of slow but steady resilience versus calculated, relentless firepower. Here’s how the battle unfolded across five intense rounds. Figur Armies Necrons – Awakened Dynasty Lokhust Lord (80 points) – Armed with the Staff of Light and the Erection Orb, this commander was ready to bolster the Necron forces and ensure they didn’t stay down for long. Skorpekh Lord (95 points) – Equipped with the Energetic Dermal Bond for added durability, he provided the muscle for his warriors. Troops: 5x Flayed Ones (60 points) – These savage creatures were set to tear into anything in their way. 5x Flayed Ones (60 points) – Another squad of the bloodthirsty Flayed Ones added extra board control. Elites: 3x Skorpekh Destroyers (90 points) – With high mobility and devastating melee, they were prepared to carve through anything in their path. Heavy Support: 2x Lokhust Heavy Destroyers (with Ementic Exterminator) – These devastating anti-tank weapons were ready to shred through armored units. Death Guard – Flyblown Host Noxious Brightbringer (90 points) Plague Surgeon (90 points) – Troops: 5x Plague Marines (60 points) – 5x Plague Marines (60 points) – Vehicles: 2x Crabs (60 points each) – Figur Deployment and Mission Objectives Mission: Main Objective: Burden of Trust Necrons (Attacker): Secondary: Cull the Horde Secondary: No Prisoners Death Guard (Defender): Secondary: Behind Enemy Lines Secondary: Engage on All Fronts Both armies prepared their forces for a slow burn of attrition, with the Death Guard on defense and the Necrons pushing aggressively. Figur Round 1: Necrons Movement: The Necron forces cautiously advanced, with the Lokhust Lord and Skorpekh Lord shifting into position. The Flayed Ones, however, seemed to stay a bit too far back. Shooting: In a turn of missed opportunities, the Necrons failed to connect with any of their shots, and the Flayed Ones were still far from engaging the enemy. Charge: The Death Guard advanced, and the Necrons attempted to charge but failed to make any impact. Death Guard (0 VPs) Movement: The Plague Marines and Crabs shuffled forward at a lethargic pace, moving only a modest 5 inches – not the ideal pace to counteract the Necron's advancing forces. Shooting: Despite the slow start, the Death Guard managed to toss a few grenades, though all shots missed their mark. Charge: The Crabs engaged the Flayed Ones, cutting down three before a surprising move from Marcellious, using one Command Point to resurrect one Flayed One.
