Warhammer 40K Battle Report: Indian Guard vs. Necrons – 500 Points, Kings of the Colosseum

By: Vore Vore

Introduction
In a grueling 500-point battle, the mighty Necrons led by marcellious. And the Depth Guard led by Elias, also known as the "Stink Guard," in a fight to dominate in Piriah Nexus. With both sides bringing powerful, yet contrasting forces to the table, it was a battle of slow but steady resilience versus calculated, relentless firepower. Here’s how the battle unfolded across five intense rounds. Armies Necrons – Awakened Dynasty

Lokhust Lord (80 points) – Armed with the Staff of Light and the Erection Orb, this commander was ready to bolster the Necron forces and ensure they didn’t stay down for long. Skorpekh Lord (95 points) – Equipped with the Energetic Dermal Bond for added durability, he provided the muscle for his warriors.

Troops: 5x Flayed Ones (60 points) – These savage creatures were set to tear into anything in their way. 5x Flayed Ones (60 points) – Another squad of the bloodthirsty Flayed Ones added extra board control. Elites: 3x Skorpekh Destroyers (90 points) – With high mobility and devastating melee, they were prepared to carve through anything in their path. Heavy Support: 2x Lokhust Heavy Destroyers (with Ementic Exterminator) – These devastating anti-tank weapons were ready to shred through armored units.

Death Guard – Flyblown Host Noxious Brightbringer (90 points) Plague Surgeon (90 points) – Troops: 5x Plague Marines (60 points) – 5x Plague Marines (60 points) – Vehicles: 2x Crabs (60 points each)

Deployment and Mission Objectives Mission: Main Objective: Burden of Trust Necrons (Attacker): Secondary: Cull the Horde Secondary: No Prisoners Death Guard (Defender): Secondary: Behind Enemy Lines Secondary: Engage on All Fronts Both armies prepared their forces for a slow burn of attrition, with the Death Guard on defense and the Necrons pushing aggressively.

Round 1:
Necrons
Movement: The Necron forces cautiously advanced, with the Lokhust Lord and Skorpekh Lord shifting into position. The Flayed Ones, however, seemed to stay a bit too far back.
Shooting: In a turn of missed opportunities, the Necrons failed to connect with any of their shots, and the Flayed Ones were still far from engaging the enemy.
Charge: The Death Guard advanced, and the Necrons attempted to charge but failed to make any impact.
Death Guard (0 VPs) Movement: The Plague Marines and Crabs shuffled forward at a lethargic pace, moving only a modest 5 inches – not the ideal pace to counteract the Necron's advancing forces.
Shooting: Despite the slow start, the Death Guard managed to toss a few grenades, though all shots missed their mark.
Charge: The Crabs engaged the Flayed Ones, cutting down three before a surprising move from Marcellious, using one Command Point to resurrect one Flayed One.

Round 2

Necrons (5 VPs)

Movement: The Skorpekh Lord and his squad surged forward, ready to wreak havoc on the Plague Marines.
Shooting: Nothing to report here – the Necrons struggled to land any significant shots.
Charge: The Necron charge was unsuccessful, and the Death Guard was able to weather the storm with their tough resilience.
Death Guard (0 VPs)

Movement: The Plague Surgeon remained stationary. Was this a tactical error, or was he simply playing it safe?
Shooting: One wound was inflicted on a Lokhust Destroyer, but it wasn’t enough to kill it.
Charge: No significant charges took place this round. The Death Guard were starting to feel the pressure.

Round 3

Necrons (15 VPs)
Movement: The backline Necron forces closed in, bringing their warriors into the fray.
Shooting: The Skorpekh Destroyers made their presence known, wiping out a full squad of Plague Marines with devastating firepower.
Charge: Despite a few attempts, none of the charges landed successfully. However, the Skorpekh Destroyers’ firepower was enough to decimate one of two Plague Marine squad’s.

Death Guard (5 VPs)
Movement: The Plague Surgeon finally moved, closing in on charge range – but was it too little, too late?
Shooting: In another round of missed shots, the Death Guard were unable to hit anything.
Charge: Nothing ever came of the charges this round, with both sides essentially at a standstill.

Round 4

Necrons (10 VPs)
Movement: The Necron forces closed in. Everything was within engagement range now.
Shooting: Some shooting took place, but much of it fell short. However, in a shocking turn of events, the Crabs were finally wiped out by the concentrated firepower of the Necrons.
Charge: The Necrons moved into assault range, but they were still unable to capitalize on it.
Death Guard (10 VPs)
Movement: The Death Guard struggled to maintain their footing, and no major movements occurred.
Shooting: There was no significant output from the Death Guard this round.
Charge: The Death Guard did manage to eliminate a squad of Flayed Ones, but the battle was slipping away from them.

Round 5

Necrons (40 VPs)
Movement: No movement, Locked in mortal combat.
Shooting: With devastating firepower, the Necrons laid waste to three Plague Marines.
Charge: We’re so back. The Plauge remaining plauge remains die.
Death Guard (20 VPs)
Movement & Shooting: There was little left for the Death Guard to do, with no meaningful shooting or movement. The Plague Marines could only stand their ground for so long.
Charge: The final remnants of the Death Guard’s forces tried to charge but were ultimately unsuccessful.

Conclusion Victory to the Necrons! With a final score of 40 VPs to the Death Guard’s 20 VPs, the Necrons took the day in the Kings of the Colosseum match.
Despite the slow and steady approach of the Death Guard, the Necrons’ relentless firepower, combined with their ability to resurrect and reanimate fallen warriors, proved too much for the Stink Guard.
The Death Guard's slow pace and ineffective charges, compounded by missed shooting opportunities, left them unable to mount a meaningful defense as the Necrons closed in for the kill.
The Necron Awakened Dynasty has once again proven why they are one of the most fearsome forces in the galaxy, securing yet another victory in the endless war for dominance in the 41st millennium.
