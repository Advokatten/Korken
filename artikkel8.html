Gardens of the Moon

Book review

The first book in the Malazan series To write a full review of gardens of the moon, I would have to had read the whole ten book series, as the white demon that made this book is also an archeologist, and he wrote this series in a way that one would have to gradually uncover the story as the books go on with minimal handholding.

So, my first review of this book is “What the fuck did I just read?”. Reading this book is the equivalent of getting your balls squished, the pressure building and it’s uncomfortable but then at that one small interval it feels kind of nice, then it reverts to pain again. The world is deliberately made to be confusing, and I don’t have the rest of the books currently to become reasonably autistic enough to make an accurate statement on this heap of exposition.

I suppose I could try to make sense of this book, but that would be the same as me trying to draw the whole story from fragments of a broken window. This book is the first in the series of ten whole books that are denser than a neutron star. So I am going to try to tell you what I’ve read, and I hope you don’t blow up.

The book is set in a war-ravaged world where peace seems distant and hate seems to be at the forefront of every conflict (much the same as our own world!) and it follows a wide range of characters. We have the group Bridgeburners, which are a specialized unit that is sent to major strategic places to disturb and destabilize the integrity of cities, most of the missions being a prep for war. Then we also have Tattersail, a mage that experiences the 9/11 equivalent of this book, then gets set out on a mission to be reborn back in time, are you following this so far??

We also have a woman that works as a normal farmhand, gets possessed by a manifestation of death itself and then goes by a new name ‘Sorry’.

We also have Paran, who is the coolest dude in the book, the dude gets killed by an assassin, gets sent to the gate of hell, then comes back alive with the Luck of Oponn (which is another set of Gods).

In this world, at this point in their time, humanity could rebel against and even kill gods. There are some things I also won’t say since that would spoil the book for you. If any of you poor sods want to actually read this fucking thing, I recommend it wholeheartedly, this book is very confusing the first time you read it but it also becomes one of the most interesting fantasy books out there.
